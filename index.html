DIFC Active Enterprise
B2BFintech Ltd Managing Office · Qualifying applicant: Eugenia Mykyliak (UBO of DFSA entity) · Activity: Managing Office
ℹ
What this entity is A DIFC Active Enterprise incorporated as the Managing Office of the B2BFintech group. It coexists in the same DIFC office as B2B Prime Services MENA Ltd (the DFSA-regulated entity). Its purpose is to coordinate group affairs, provide intra-group services (IT, admin, operations) and sponsor the ~50 employees being transferred from B2BMENA (mainland) upon its liquidation.
⚠
Qualifying logic — read carefully Eugenia Mykyliak qualifies as the applicant under DIFC-CS-HB-08 Rev.02, p.2, rule (c): "a shareholder or an Ultimate Beneficial Owner that Controls a DIFC Registered Entity." The DFSA entity (B2B Prime Services MENA Ltd) is the DIFC Registered Entity whose existence makes Eugenia a qualifying UBO. The DFSA entity does NOT control or own the new AE — Eugenia does, in her personal capacity as UBO. The AE and DFSA entity are sister entities sharing common UBO and office space only.

Why This Structure Works
✅
Eugenia qualifies the AE
She is UBO of B2B Prime Services MENA Ltd, a DIFC Registered Entity. This satisfies rule (c) of the Active Enterprise qualifying criteria.
Source: DIFC-CS-HB-08 Rev.02, p.2 & p.6
✅
B2BFintech Cyprus as shareholder
The handbook separates "qualifying controller" from "shareholder." B2BFintech Cyprus holds 100% of shares; Eugenia is the qualifying controller on the portal. These are different roles.
Source: DIFC-CS-HB-08 Rev.02, p.3 (Shareholders section)
✅
Shared office is explicitly permitted
The AE can "share office space with its DIFC affiliate." Eugenia's common UBO makes the DFSA entity and AE affiliates. A Letter of Consent from the DFSA entity (leaseholder) is required.
Source: DIFC-CS-HB-08 Rev.02, p.5 (Affiliated Entities section)
✅
Managing Office activity is valid
Managing Office is an explicitly listed activity for Active Enterprises. It covers coordinating administrative, operational and strategic functions of affiliated companies.
Source: DIFC-CS-HB-08 Rev.02, p.2-3; AE brochure p.2

For a Managing Office, the name must include a word indicating management or coordination.

Shared Office Space
The DIFC Active Enterprise can share the DFSA entity's existing office. Conditions are documented in the official handbook.
✅
Explicitly permitted by the handbook DIFC-CS-HB-08 Rev.02, p.5: "If your Private Company will be sharing office space with another affiliated entity in DIFC, upload a letter of consent for sharing office space issued by the affiliated entity in DIFC. The entity issuing the letter must be the leaseholder or the owner of the property."
Conditions for Shared Space
✅
Affiliate relationship confirmed
The handbook permits sharing with a "DIFC affiliate." The DFSA entity and the new AE are affiliates by virtue of Eugenia's common UBO. The "Affiliated Entities" section of the portal requires you to declare this relationship.
Source: DIFC-CS-HB-08 Rev.02, p.5 (Affiliated Entities)
📄
Letter of Consent required
The DFSA entity (as leaseholder) must issue a Letter of Consent for sharing office space. This must be uploaded during the AE registration process. Without this, the shared address cannot be used as the AE's registered address.
Source: DIFC-CS-HB-08 Rev.02, p.5 (Document: Letter of Consent)
🏢
Address appears automatically on portal
The handbook states: "If you are sharing office space with an affiliate, the registered address of the affiliated entity will appear." The lease must be registered with the DIFC Registrar of Real Property to be visible.
Source: DIFC-CS-HB-08 Rev.02, p.7 (Registered Address)
⚠
Operational separation still required
Sharing physical space does not remove the DFSA's expectation of operational separation between the regulated entity and the non-regulated AE. Separate IT systems, client data, financial records, and documented information barriers are required.
Source: DFSA GEN Rulebook (general principle); DFSA-regulated firm obligations
⚠
Visa quota — unresolved
Whether two entities sharing one DIFC office share one visa quota pool (based on sq ft) or each have their own quota from the same sq footage is NOT resolved by available sources. Must be confirmed with DIFC before planning headcount.

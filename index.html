The material difference is structural, not quantum: mainland creates a growing lump-sum gratuity liability on the balance sheet payable at termination; DIFC DEWS converts this into a predictable monthly cash cost (5.83% of basic for years 1–5, 8.33% for years 6+) that is portable for employees. DFSA Entity Impact
What changes for B2B Prime Services MENA Ltd (the existing DFSA-regulated entity) as a result of establishing the new DIFC Active Enterprise alongside it.
🚨
Act before incorporating the AE The DFSA entity must review its license conditions and, if required, notify the DFSA before the AE is incorporated. Proceeding without required notification is a regulatory breach. This is the single most time-sensitive pre-condition in the entire project.
Obligations Triggered by AE Incorporation
🔔
DFSA Close Links Notification
Eugenia Mykyliak's common UBO position across the DFSA entity and the new AE constitutes a "close link" under DFSA GEN Rulebook. DFSA-regulated firms are required to notify the DFSA of close links that may affect its ability to supervise the regulated firm. The DFSA entity's specific license conditions must be reviewed to determine whether notification, prior consent, or a no-objection letter is required before proceeding.
⚠ ACTION REQUIRED — Review license conditions first
DFSA GEN Rulebook, Rule 5.3 (Close Links); DIFC Incorporation Plan, Section 7.1
📄
Letter of Consent for Shared Office
The DFSA entity, as leaseholder of the shared DIFC office, must issue a Letter of Consent permitting the AE to use its address as a registered office. This is a mandatory upload during the AE's Stage 2 registration. The DFSA entity's board or authorised signatory must approve and sign this letter.
⚠ ACTION REQUIRED — Must be prepared before AE registration
DIFC-CS-HB-08 Rev.02, p.5 (Affiliated Entities / Letter of Consent)
🔒
Operational Separation Requirements
The DFSA expects regulated firms sharing premises with affiliated non-regulated entities to maintain clear operational separation. This requires: separate IT infrastructure and data environments; no commingling of client data or confidential information from the regulated business; separate bank accounts and financial reporting; a documented Information Barrier Policy covering all staff who work across both entities.
⚠ ACTION REQUIRED — Before any staff start working across both entities
DFSA GEN Rulebook (general principle); DIFC Incorporation Plan, Section 7.2
📋
Management Services Agreement
A formal Management Services Agreement (MSA) or Technology Services Agreement (TSA) between the AE and the DFSA entity is strongly recommended. It must cover: scope of services provided; arm's-length pricing (transfer pricing rules apply to related-party transactions); IP ownership and licensing; data handling and confidentiality; liability and indemnification. The DFSA may review this agreement as part of its ongoing supervision.
→ Recommended before AE commences operations
DIFC Incorporation Plan, Section 7.3; UAE Corporate Tax transfer pricing rules
💼
Dual-Role Staff — Disclosure Obligations
If any individuals hold roles or perform functions in both the DFSA entity and the AE, this must be disclosed to the DFSA. Approved Person status at the DFSA entity may be affected. Each individual's responsibilities must be clearly delineated and documented.
→ Review before making any appointments
DFSA GEN Rulebook (Approved Persons); DFSA COB Module
🏦
Corporate Tax — Inter-Company Transactions
Transactions between the AE (Managing Office) and the DFSA entity (related party) are subject to UAE Corporate Tax transfer pricing rules. All pricing must be at arm's length and documented. The DFSA entity's income from or payments to the AE must be correctly characterised for tax purposes. Both entities should have UAE CT registered and file separately.
→ Engage UAE CT advisor before MSA is signed
UAE Corporate Tax Law (Federal Decree-Law No. 47 of 2022), transfer pricing provisions
B2BFintech Ltd (Cyprus) — Shareholder Obligations
B2BFintech Ltd (Cyprus) is the 100% shareholder of the new DIFC Active Enterprise. These are its specific obligations and preparation steps.
ℹ
Shareholder ≠ Qualifying Controller B2BFintech Cyprus is the shareholder of the AE — it holds 100% of the shares. It is NOT the qualifying controller on the DIFC portal. Eugenia Mykyliak is the qualifying person (UBO). These are two separate concepts in the DIFC Active Enterprise framework. B2BFintech Cyprus does not need to be a DIFC entity or have any DIFC nexus of its own — its role is purely as equity holder.
What B2BFintech Cyprus Must Prepare
1
Board Resolution for DIFC AE Incorporation
The Cyprus board must pass a resolution that: (a) authorises the incorporation of the DIFC Active Enterprise; (b) states that the Articles of Association have been duly adopted; (c) appoints the authorised signatories to appear on the DIFC license; (d) appoints the person authorised to sign the Articles of Association. The resolution must not be older than 6 months from the date of DIFC approval. A template is available on the DIFC portal.
DIFC-CS-HB-08 Rev.02, p.9-10 (Resolution of board of directors)
2
Certificate of Incorporation
A certified copy of B2BFintech Cyprus's Certificate of Incorporation (or equivalent document) must be uploaded to the DIFC portal. It must be certified by the issuing authority. If the certification is not electronic, it must not be older than 6 months. No UAE Embassy attestation is required — DIFC does not require attestation for corporate documents.
DIFC-CS-HB-08 Rev.02, p.4 (Certificate of Incorporation for Body Corporate Shareholder); AE brochure p.1
3
2-Year Audited Financial Statements
As the entity funding the DIFC AE, B2BFintech Cyprus must provide its 2-year audited financial statements to evidence source of funds. If 2-year audited financials are not available (e.g., entity is relatively new), the most recent 1-year bank statements may be accepted at DIFC's discretion.
DIFC-CS-HB-08 Rev.02, p.6 (Sources of Income / Financial statements)
4
Share Class Definition and Allocation
On the DIFC portal, define the share class (e.g., Ordinary shares) and nominal value, then allocate 100% of issued shares to B2BFintech Cyprus. All issued shares must be allocated to approved shareholders as part of the initial approval submission.
DIFC-CS-HB-08 Rev.02, p.4 (Share Class; Allocate shares)
5
Notarised PoA (if signatory is not a company officer)
If the person signing the DIFC Articles of Association on behalf of B2BFintech Cyprus is not a director or company officer of that entity, a notarised and valid Power of Attorney is required. This is only needed if the authorised signatory is different from the official representatives identified in the board resolution.
DIFC-CS-HB-08 Rev.02, p.4 (Document: Notarised PoA)
6
Cyprus Regulatory Considerations (CySEC / AML)
Although the AE is a non-regulated entity, B2BFintech Cyprus (as a Cyprus-incorporated entity under Eugenia's ownership) should consider whether establishing and owning a DIFC Managing Office triggers any reporting obligations under CySEC regulations or Cyprus AML/beneficial ownership filing requirements. This is a matter for Cyprus counsel — DIFC does not require any Cyprus regulatory approvals.
Cyprus AML Law (transposing EU 5AMLD/6AMLD); Cyprus Companies Law, Cap. 113

Conditions and Requirements
✅
Affiliate relationship established
The handbook permits sharing with a "DIFC affiliate." The DFSA entity and AE are affiliates by virtue of Eugenia's common UBO. The portal's "Affiliated Entities" section requires declaring this relationship and the intent to share space.
DIFC-CS-HB-08 Rev.02, p.5
📄
Letter of Consent — mandatory upload
The DFSA entity (as leaseholder) must issue and sign a Letter of Consent. This is a mandatory document upload in Stage 2 of the AE registration. Without it, the shared address cannot be used as the AE's registered office.
DIFC-CS-HB-08 Rev.02, p.5 (Document: Letter of Consent)
🏢
Registered address appears automatically on portal
The handbook states: "If you are sharing office space with an affiliate, the registered address of the affiliated entity will appear." The lease must be registered with the DIFC Registrar of Real Property. If the lease is with DIFC Investments Ltd (co-working), it is registered automatically by the landlord.
DIFC-CS-HB-08 Rev.02, p.7 (Registered Address)
⚠
Operational separation still required despite shared space
Physical co-location does not remove the DFSA's expectation of operational separation between the regulated entity and the non-regulated AE. Separate IT systems, client data environments, financial records, and a documented Information Barrier Policy are required.
DFSA GEN Rulebook; DIFC Incorporation Plan, Section 7.2
